# Unveiling SPWS Designer: A Revolutionary Tool for Water Supply Engineers

In the realm of water supply engineering, the pursuit of sustainable, efficient, and economically viable solutions remains a top priority. The SPWS Designer, a web-based decision support tool, emerges as a beacon for engineers in Malawi, offering a sophisticated platform to evaluate and design water supply systems. This comprehensive blog post will delve into the intricacies of SPWS Designer, exploring its architecture, features, and potential impact on the water supply sector.

## The Problem at Hand

Water supply challenges are particularly pronounced in regions like Malawi, where access to clean and reliable water sources can significantly impact public health and quality of life. Engineers face the daunting task of designing systems that are not only effective and sustainable but also economically viable over the long term. Traditional boreholes with handpumps, while common, may not always provide the most efficient or scalable solution. There's a growing need for innovative approaches that can leverage renewable energy sources, such as solar power, to enhance water distribution systems.

This is where the SPWS Designer steps in. It provides engineers with a platform to compare two key water supply technologies:
1. **Solar Piped Water Systems (SPWS)**: These systems centralize water collection and distribution using solar-powered pumps, elevated tanks, and a network of pipes leading to tap stands.
2. **Boreholes with Handpumps**: A decentralized approach where individual handpumps serve as point sources for water collection.

By facilitating a comprehensive analysis of these systems, the SPWS Designer ensures that engineers can make informed decisions that align with both local needs and global sustainability goals.

## Understanding the SPWS Designer's Architecture

### Frontend Technology

The SPWS Designer's frontend is crafted using modern web development technologies to ensure a seamless and interactive user experience.

- **Framework**: The tool leverages [React](https://react.dev/) in conjunction with [Vite](https://vitejs.dev/), offering a fast and efficient development environment. React's component-based architecture allows for a dynamic and responsive interface, crucial for the tool's interactive design map feature.
- **Language**: TypeScript is employed for enhanced type safety and robust logic, minimizing runtime errors and improving code maintainability.
- **Styling**: The UI is styled using [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com/), a utility-first CSS framework that allows for rapid customization and a consistent design language across the app.

### Mapping and Geospatial Capabilities

SPWS Designer's geospatial capabilities are pivotal to its functionality, providing users with detailed mapping and spatial planning tools.

- **Engine**: [Leaflet](https://leafletjs.com/) is the mapping engine of choice, known for its lightweight and flexible design. It effectively supports the rendering of dynamic maps with various layers.
- **Data Formats**:
  - **FlatGeobuf**: This format allows the tool to efficiently stream millions of building polygons without requiring a backend server, critical for population estimation.
  - **Cloud Optimized GeoTIFF (COG)**: Used to serve raster layers such as elevation and groundwater potential, these are stored as static files to enhance performance.
  - **OpenStreetMap**: Provides comprehensive base maps and geocoding capabilities, ensuring accurate location searches and overlays.

### Serverless Backend

A significant innovation within the SPWS Designer is its serverless backend, reducing overhead and maintenance burdens.

- **Compute**: The tool utilizes [Google Apps Script](https://script.google.com/) for backend logic, a cloud-based scripting environment that allows for web app deployment and automation.
- **Database**: [Google Sheets](https://sheets.google.com/) serves as the database, offering a familiar and accessible platform for data storage and retrieval.
- **Integration**: The frontend communicates with the backend via `fetch()` requests, logging designs and user feedback efficiently.

## Features that Set SPWS Designer Apart

### Interactive Design Map

The interactive design map is a standout feature, providing engineers with real-time visualization tools to aid in decision-making.

- **Satellite and Topographic Views**: Users can switch between satellite imagery and topographic views to better assess the terrain and environmental factors affecting water supply design.
- **Google Building Footprints**: By incorporating over 6 million building footprints, the tool enables accurate population estimation, a crucial factor in system design.
- **Hydrogeology Layers**: Visualizations of "Depth to Water" and "Groundwater Potential" guide engineers in optimal borehole siting, enhancing the feasibility and sustainability of designs.

### Hydraulic Engine

The hydraulic engine is the backbone of the SPWS Designer, performing complex calculations to optimize system designs.

- **Dynamic Pipe Sizing and Friction Loss Calculations**: The tool dynamically sizes pipes and calculates friction losses based on user input and geospatial data.
- **Pump Power and Solar Array Sizing**: It determines the necessary pump power and solar array size using Total Dynamic Head (TDH) calculations, ensuring efficient energy use.
- **Pipeline Elevation Profiling**: By profiling pipeline elevation, the tool assists in identifying potential challenges and optimizing system efficiency.

### Economic Analysis

The economic analysis component provides a comprehensive financial overview of proposed systems.

- **20-Year Lifecycle Cost Analysis (LCCA)**: This analysis estimates the total cost of ownership, including capital and operational expenditures over two decades.
- **Benefit Monetization**: The tool calculates the economic value of time savings, health improvements, and carbon credits, offering a holistic view of system benefits.
- **Monte Carlo Simulation**: Running 10,000 scenarios, the simulation assesses risk and probability of success, providing confidence in decision-making.

### PDF Reporting

The ability to generate professional feasibility reports directly in the browser ensures that engineers can present their findings effectively to stakeholders and donors.

## Getting Started with SPWS Designer

### Prerequisites

To embark on your journey with SPWS Designer, ensure that you have Node.js (version 18 or higher) installed on your system. This will facilitate the installation and running of the tool locally.

### Installation Process

1. **Clone the Repository**: Begin by cloning the SPWS1 repository from GitHub.
   ```bash
   git clone https://github.com/washways/SPWS1.git
   cd SPWS1
   ```
2. **Install Dependencies**: Run the following command to install necessary dependencies.
   ```bash
   npm install
   ```
3. **Run Locally**: Start the development server with the command below, then open `http://localhost:5173` in your browser.
   ```bash
   npm run dev
   ```

### Backend Setup

For those forking the repository or setting it up anew, connecting to a Google Apps Script backend is essential. This setup is crucial for logging data and managing feedback.

1. **Create a Google Sheet**: Set up a new sheet to log data.
2. **Add the Provided Script**: Insert the script as outlined in the project's documentation.
3. **Deploy as a Web App**: Ensure that access is set to "Anyone" to facilitate seamless integration.
4. **Update Configuration**: Modify the `GOOGLE_SCRIPT_URL` in `src/services/analyticsService.ts` with your deployment URL.

## How SPWS Designer Works: A Detailed Workflow

1. **Locate & Identify**: Users begin by searching for a village. The tool leverages building footprints to estimate the population, typically assuming five people per household.
2. **Design System**: Engineers can place boreholes, tanks, and draw pipes to connect taps throughout the village, visualizing the entire water distribution network.
3. **Hydraulic Check**: The tool computes elevation differences using the Open-Meteo API, calculates pipe friction, and determines the required pump power.
4. **Financial Inputs**: Users confirm costs, including capital and operational expenditures, tariffs, and subsidy levels.
5. **Simulate**: The economic analysis tab compares the 20-year Net Present Value (NPV) of the solar system against multiple handpumps, providing a clear financial comparison.
6. **Report**: A comprehensive PDF report can be generated for presentation to stakeholders and donors, summarizing the analysis and design.

## Future Enhancements: Pioneering Features on the Horizon

### Offline Capability (PWA)

Field engineers often operate in areas with limited internet access. Converting the SPWS Designer into a Progressive Web App (PWA) could provide offline capabilities, caching static assets and map tiles for use in the field, and syncing data with Google Sheets when connectivity is restored.

### Advanced Hydraulic Solver

Integrating a compiled [EPANET](https://github.com/OpenWaterAnalytics/epanet-js) WebAssembly module could enhance the tool's ability to handle looped networks and complex pressure zones, overcoming the limitations of its current simple branching network assumption.

### User Accounts & Projects

To preserve designs beyond individual sessions, integrating user authentication via Firebase or Supabase could allow users to save and load their projects, facilitating ongoing project management.

### Live GEE Integration

Reintegrating Google Earth Engine for dynamic layer generation could expand the tool's applicability beyond Malawi, offering global coverage and real-time data processing capabilities.
