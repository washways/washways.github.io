A satellite-powered screening tool for mapping groundwater potential in data-scarce regions. Combines topography, climate, soil, vegetation, and structural proxies into a single 0–10 index.

How to Use

  1. Zoom in to a district or catchment (zoom level ≥ 9 recommended)
  2. Adjust pillar weights if needed (Storage / Supply / Yield)
  3. Set the dry-season NDVI month for your region (default: September for Malawi)
  4. Click RUN to compute the Groundwater Potential Index
  5. Click any point on the map for a detailed diagnostic popup
  6. Toggle pillar layers in the Layers panel to understand what drives the score
  7. Enable Auto-stretch for better colour contrast at local scale

Methodology: Source–Pathway–Receptor

The index combines three independently normalised pillars (0–1) using user-adjustable weights, then applies a multiplicative terrain constraint. The final score ranges from 0 (very low) to 10 (exceptional).

Storage (Receptor)

Estimates saturated regolith thickness as DTB − HAND. Deep weathered rock below the drainage base-level means more aquifer volume. Log-normalised to 0–1.

Supply (Source)

Combines Aridity Index (climate moisture surplus) with upstream drainage area (topographic water concentration). Wetter climate + larger catchment = more recharge.

Yield (Pathway)

Blends five proxies: fracture lineaments, topographic convergence, dry-season NDVI (phreatophyte signal), soil transmissivity, and lithology factor.

Constraint (Penalty)

A multiplicative decay from slope and HAND. Steep rocky terrain → score collapses to zero, no matter how high the pillars. Rejects the "additive fallacy".

Score Interpretation

ScoreClassGuidance
0 – 2.0 Very Low Low combined evidence. De-prioritise unless strong local knowledge exists.
2.0 – 3.5 Low Some signal, but weak. Use component layers + boreholes to justify.
3.5 – 5.0 Medium Promising. Identify the driving pillar(s) and verify locally.
5.0 – 6.2 High Strong screening signal. Good candidate for field verification.
6.2 – 10 Exceptional Top-tier signal. Still verify geology, siting constraints, and water quality.

Data Sources

  • MERIT Hydro v1.0.1 Elevation, HAND, Upstream Area — 90 m
  • Depth to Bedrock (DTB) Shangguan et al. — 250 m
  • Global Aridity Index v3.1 Zomer et al. — ~1 km
  • Sentinel-2 SR Harmonized Copernicus / ESA — 10 m
  • SoilGrids v2 (ISRIC) Clay, Sand, CFVO, Bulk Density — 250 m
  • FAO GAUL 2015 Administrative boundaries (export only)
